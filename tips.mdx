## Quick Add Mastery

The fastest way to create tasks! 

**Basic Syntax**
```text
"Task name"
```

### Priority Tags

| Syntax | Priority |
|--------|----------|
| `!p1`  | Critical |
| `!p2`  | High     |
| `!p3`  | Medium   |
| `!p4`  | Low      |

### Subject Tags

```text
#SubjectName
#Math
#Physics
#Chemistry
```

### Due Dates

| Syntax      | Result      |
|-------------|-------------|
| `@today`    | Today       |
| `@tomorrow` | Tomorrow    |
| `@monday`   | Next Monday |
| `@15/02`    | Feb 15      |
| `@nextweek` | Next week   |

**Example Usage:**
```text
Study Physics Ch.5 !p1 #Physics @friday
Math homework !p2 #Math @tomorrow
Review notes !p3 #Chemistry @nextweek
```

## Task Management Tips

<Note>
  **The Two-Minute Rule**
  If a task takes **less than 2 minutes**, do it immediately! *(This keeps your task list from getting cluttered).*
</Note>

### Batching Similar Tasks
Group tasks by Subject, Energy level, or Location. 
*Example: "Do all Math tasks in one batch."*

### Using the Eisenhower Matrix
Prioritize effectively with this method:
- **Do First** - Urgent + Important
- **Schedule** - Not urgent + Important
- **Delegate** - Urgent + Not important
- **Eliminate** - Not urgent + Not important

### Subtask Power
Break big tasks into manageable subtasks:
1. Create main task
2. Add subtasks
3. Check off individually for satisfying progress

## Study Workflows

### Daily Routine

<Steps>
  <Step title="Morning (10 min)">
    Check Dashboard, review AI insights, and plan today's priorities.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Between Classes (25 min)">
    Quick focus session and review flashcards.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Evening (1-2 hours)">
    Deep study session focusing on new material.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Before Bed (15 min)">
    Light revision, plan for tomorrow, and check off completed tasks.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Exam Prep Workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="8 weeks before">
    Load exam syllabus and create your overarching study schedule.
  </Step>
  <Step title="6 weeks before">
    Start mock tests and identify/focus on weak areas.
  </Step>
  <Step title="4 weeks before">
    Increase mock frequency and focus heavily on revision.
  </Step>
  <Step title="2 weeks before">
    Light study only, practice tests daily, prioritize rest and mental prep.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Weekly Review
Every Sunday:
1. Check Analytics
2. Review week's progress
3. Plan next week
4. Adjust goals

## Productivity Hacks

- **The Pomodoro Variation:** Try Ultra Focus (50m work / 10m break), Quick Sprint (15m work / 3m break), or Deep Dive (90m work / 20m break).
- **Energy-Based Scheduling:** Match tasks to your energy. High energy = New topics. Medium energy = Review. Low energy = Organization/planning.
- **The Seinfeld Strategy:** Don't break the chain! Use the streak feature and aim for consistency. Even 15 minutes counts.
- **Spaced Repetition:** Use SRS effectively. Review daily, don't skip reviews, rate honestly, and trust the system.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

<Warning>
  **The 5 Don'ts of Isotope AI:**
  1. **Don't skip reviews** - SRS needs consistency to build retention.
  2. **Don't create too many tasks** - Focus on quality over volume.
  3. **Don't ignore analytics** - Data helps you improve your habits.
  4. **Don't study without focus** - Always use the timer.
  5. **Don't skip breaks** - Rest is a productive part of learning.
</Warning>
